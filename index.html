Other Fun Things to Do

🐋 Whale Watching

April is the tail end of humpback whale season on Maui! Thousands of humpbacks migrate from Alaska to Hawaii's warm waters each winter, and in early April you can still catch mothers and calves lingering close to shore as the babies build strength before the journey north. You might spot them breaching right from the beach, or book a whale watching tour out of Ma'alaea Harbor (just 15 min from Wailea). Keep your eyes on the water!

🌅 Haleakala Sunrise

Watch the sunrise from 10,023 feet above sea level at the summit of Haleakala. It's one of the most iconic things to do on Maui. Fair warning: it's about a 1.5 hour drive from Wailea, you need to leave around 3:30am, and it's genuinely cold at the top (30-40°F). Bring layers!

Reservation
Required. Book at recreation.gov. They sell out weeks in advance.
Entry fee
$1.50 reservation + $30/vehicle (National Park)
Tip
Bring jackets, blankets, and hot coffee. It's freezing up there.

Good to Know

🚗 Rental Cars

Book your rental car ASAP if you haven't already. Maui cars sell out, especially in spring. Uber and Lyft exist but are unreliable outside Kahului. You'll want a car.

🛒 Stock Up at Costco

There's a Costco right by the Kahului airport. Grab groceries, drinks, and sunscreen on your way to the condo. Prices at resort shops and ABC Stores are steep.

🕐 Time Zone

Hawaii is 3 hours behind Pacific time in April (Hawaii doesn't observe daylight saving). Plan accordingly if you need to call home or join any work calls.

☀️ Sun & Sunscreen

The UV here is intense, even on cloudy days. Reapply often. Hawaii law requires reef-safe sunscreen (mineral-based only: zinc oxide or titanium dioxide). Common mainland brands like Banana Boat and Coppertone spray don't qualify. You can find compliant brands at any ABC Store or Long's Drugs on the island.

🌊 Ocean Safety

Conditions change daily. Always check for posted warnings before swimming, never turn your back on the waves, and keep an eye on kids near the water. South Maui beaches (Wailea/Kihei) are generally calmer, but respect the ocean.

🐢 Respect the Wildlife

Sea turtles, Hawaiian monk seals, and humpback whales are all federally protected. Stay at least 10 feet from turtles and seals on the beach, and 100 yards from whales in the water. Look, don't touch!

💵 Cash

Some local food spots and shave ice stands are cash only. Good idea to have some on hand.
